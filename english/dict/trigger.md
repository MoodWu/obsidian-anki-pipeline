# trigger

/ˈtrɪɡər/

## Meaning
verb
To cause something to start or happen, especially a series of events.
引发，触发（尤指一系列事件）

noun
A small device that releases a spring or catch and so sets off a mechanism, especially in order to fire a gun.
（尤指枪的）扳机

noun
Something that causes a strong emotional reaction or a particular memory, especially because it is connected with a traumatic experience.
（尤指因与创伤经历有关而引发强烈情绪反应或特定记忆的）诱因，起因

## Example
The loud noise triggered a panic among the crowd.

## Translation
巨大的噪音在人群中引发了恐慌。

## Cloze
The sight of the old photograph ____ a flood of childhood memories.

## Note
English: The word 'trigger' originates from the Dutch 'trekker', meaning 'to pull'. It is commonly used both as a noun (the part of a gun) and a verb (to cause something to happen). In psychology, it often refers to something that causes a negative emotional response.
中文：单词 'trigger' 源于荷兰语 'trekker'，意为“拉”。它通常既用作名词（枪的扳机），也用作动词（引发某事）。在心理学中，它常指引发负面情绪反应的事物。
