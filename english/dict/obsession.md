# obsession

/əbˈseʃn/

## Meaning
noun
A persistent disturbing preoccupation with an often unreasonable idea or feeling; broadly: compelling motivation.
一种持续不断的、令人不安的对某个常常不合理的想法或感觉的沉迷；广义上指：强烈的动机。

noun
An idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind.
一个不断占据或侵入人脑海的想法或念头。

## Example
His obsession with cleanliness made it difficult for him to relax in his own home.

## Translation
他对清洁的痴迷使得他在自己家里都很难放松下来。

## Cloze
She developed an unhealthy ____ with social media, checking her phone every few minutes.

## Note
词根词缀：来自拉丁语 obsessio，意为“围攻，困扰”，由 ob- (反对，在...前面) + sedere (坐) 构成。同根词：obsess (v. 使着迷), obsessive (adj. 着迷的), obsessively (adv. 着迷地)。
Word formation: From Latin obsessio, meaning 'siege, blockade', composed of ob- (against, in front of) + sedere (to sit). Related words: obsess (v.), obsessive (adj.), obsessively (adv.).
